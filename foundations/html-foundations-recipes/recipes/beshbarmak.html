Beshbarmak

Description

Beshbarmak is the most iconic Kazakh dish, traditionally eaten with the hands,
which is why its name translates to "five fingers." It is a celebratory meal
consisting of large, thin sheets of hand-rolled pasta topped with tender,
slow-boiled meat (usually horse or lamb) and plenty of onions cooked in a
rich meat broth called shyk.

Ingredients

  • 2 kg Meat (Lamb, Beef, or Horse meat)
  • 500g Flour
  • 2 Eggs
  • 3-4 Large Onions
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water or broth

Steps

  1. Boil the meat in a large pot for 2-3 hours until tender, skimming off the fat.
  2. Prepare the dough by mixing flour, eggs, and a little broth. Roll it out very thin and cut into large squares.
  3. Remove the cooked meat and slice it into bite-sized pieces.
  4. Boil the dough squares in the meat broth for a few minutes until cooked.
  5. Saut√© sliced onions in some of the meat fat and broth.
  6. Layer the dough on a large platter, top with the meat, and pour the onion mixture over everything.
